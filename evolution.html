    • 60's

    In 1962, J.C.R. Licklider created the concept for a global network of computers. The plan for ARPANET also gets published by Lawrence G. Roberts. A computer network became a reality in 1969 and ARPANET grew.

    70's

    In 1973, Robert Kahn and Vinton Cerf collaborate to develop a protocol to be used in linking multiple networks. This is later on what is now known as the TCP/IP. Robert Metcalfe develops a system using cables to transfer more data over a network, this was first known as Alto Aloha but is now known as Ethernet. Hypertext is suggested by Ted Nelson to be used to organize network information. A Unix-based system, called USENET is developed, to transfer data over phone lines via a dial-up connection

    80's

    In 1981, Metcalfe's company 3Com announces Ethernet products for personal and workstation computers, and this allowed the establishment of Local Area Networks. In 1982 ARPANET gets expanded with the addition of the PhoneNet system. Telenet is created, becoming the first commercial network. Different nations were able to communicate via emails. 1985 saw the first-ever registered domain, symbolics.com

    90's

    In 1990 ARPANET is decommissioned. HTML and URL is developed, bringing the first incarnation of the World Wide Web. In 1995 Microsoft launches Windows 95; Yahoo, eBay, Amazon all launch; Java is created; Internet Explorer is also launched. In 1996 CDA(Communications Decency Act) is passed in congress. 1998 saw the founding of Google. In 1999 Napster was launched and the piracy controversy intensified. 1999 also saw the first internet virus

    00's

    Google sees a meteoric rise in domination of the search engine market. Wi-Fi sees its rise as well as mobile internet devices like smartphones. 2005 sees the first-ever internet cat video.

    By Keanu Ver Burgh, Coding Comrades. 2021
